The Top Categories of Comprehensive Cat Breeds

  • Abyssinian


    The Abyssinian cat is one of the oldest cat breeds in the world. They look like little pumas and their special feature is their “ticked” coat (also called “agouti”).

  • American Shorthair


    The American Shorthair is larger, slimmer and stronger than its relative, the British Shorthair. It has a calm, friendly and very well-balanced character.

  • Balinese



    Balinese cats are playful, curious and constantly up to something. At the same time, they’re loving, loyal and sensitive.

  • Birman

    The Birman, also known as the Sacred Birman, is a medium-sized cat breed with a half-length coat and a cute, soft voice. Its special features are its white paws that are also called white socks.

  • British Shorthair

    The British Shorthair is quiet, easy-going and even-tempered. The British Shorthair is more down to earth. It might be less active and playful but it does radiate coziness, relaxation, dependability and stability.

The Top Categories of Hybrid Designer Cat Breeds

  • Chartreux

    The Chartreux is one of the friendliest cat breeds in the world! They’re especially joyful and have a great sense of humor. Sometimes, they can be downright goofy.

  • Exotic Shorthair


    The Exotic Shorthair cat looks very similar to the Persian. he Exotic Shorthair cat has a very cute, gentle, calm, friendly and loving character.

  • Maine Coon



    The Maine Coon is the largest and heaviest cat breed in the world. They are often described as “gentle giants”.

  • Norwegian Forest Cat

    The Norwegian Forest Cat looks wild and untamable but is actually a very well-balanced, gentle, loving and friendly cat. No matter how hectic life gets, nothing upsets this unflappable cat.

  • Persian


    They move and behave so elegantly and gracefully that you constantly have the feeling that you should address them as “Your Majesty”.

  • Ragdoll

    They're gentle, large cat with brilliant, light blue eyes. They have one particularly special characteristic: when you lift up a Ragdoll cat, it will let its body go limp like a ragdoll.

  • Russian Blue

    The Russian Blue is a gentle, calm, peaceful and even-tempered cat. They seem graceful and genteel thanks to their shimmering gray-blue to silver fur, their slim bodies and their elegant movements.

  • Savannah

    The Savannah cat, or just Savannah, is a large slim cat with long legs and exotic fur markings. It comes from a cross between a house cat and a serval, an African wild cat.

  • Scottish Fold

    The first thing you notice about these cats is their ears. They hang forwards as if they’ve been folded like a sheet of paper. Its cute face is often compared with that of an owl or a teddy bear.

  • Siamese


    Siamese cats are elegant, svelte cats with short fur. But it’s not just their lovely look that makes them so extraordinary. It’s their character!

The Top Categories of Classic Hound Cat Breeds

  • Siberian


    The Siberian cat is a large, powerful cat with a gorgeously fluffy half-length coat that protected them from the icy cold in their home country of Russia.

  • Turkish Angora

    The Turkish Angora is a cute, elegant and very resolute cat with high intelligence. By the way: they are the oldest long-haired cat breed, kind of making them the “mothers” of all long-haired cats.

  • Sphynx

    The Sphynx (also sometimes spelled sphinx) is a cat without fur. So they’re kind of naked and you can clearly see their skin wrinkles. This makes them a bit of a shock, if not downright scary, for some.

  • Turkish Van

    The Turkish Van is a very old, large and powerful cat breed with long hair and special markings. They always have white fur with markings only on the head and tail.

  • York Chocolate


    The fur of the York Chocolate is its special highlight: it’s medium-length with a charming chocolate brown color, and feels as silky soft as down!
