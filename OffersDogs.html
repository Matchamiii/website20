Top Services That We Offers For Dogs

  • Veterinary Consultation (General Check-Up)

    A standard veterinary consultation includes a thorough physical examination, discussion of your dog’s health, and recommendations for diet, vaccinations, or further diagnostics. The vet will check your dog’s weight, heart rate, teeth, coat condition, and overall wellness. Follow-up visits may be required for chronic conditions.

    Price:50–100 per visit

  • Vaccinations (Core & Non-Core Vaccines)

    Vaccinations protect dogs from life-threatening diseases. Core vaccines are legally required, while non-core vaccines depend on lifestyle (e.g., boarding, hiking). Puppies need a series of shots, and adults require boosters every 1–3 years.

    Price: Core Vaccines (Rabies, Distemper, Parvovirus, Adenovirus): 20–40 per shot Non-Core Vaccines (Bordetella, Leptospirosis, Lyme): 25–50 per shot

  • Full Grooming (Bath, Haircut, Brushing, Ear Cleaning)

    A full grooming session includes a bath with dog-friendly shampoo, blow-drying, brushing to remove tangles, nail trimming, ear cleaning, and a breed-specific haircut (if needed). Helps maintain coat health, reduce shedding, and prevent skin infections.

    Price: 50–120 (varies by breed & coat type)

  • Nail Trimming

    Overgrown nails can cause pain and posture issues. A professional trim ensures nails are cut safely without hitting the quick (blood vessel). Some groomers offer grinding for smoother edges.

    Price:10–25

    X-Ray (Radiography)

    X-rays help diagnose fractures, tumors, lung/heart conditions, or foreign object ingestion. May require sedation for anxious dogs. Additional fees apply for interpretation by a radiologist.

    Price:150–400 per session

  • Euthanasia (In-Clinic or At-Home)

    A humane and painless procedure performed by a vet when a dog’s quality of life declines. Includes sedation, final moments with the owner, and peaceful passing. Some vets offer paw prints or fur clippings as keepsakes.

    In-Clinic: 100–300 At-Home: 200 –500 (more for travel & comfort care)

  • Cremation (Private or Communal)

    After euthanasia, owners can choose cremation. Private cremation ensures only your dog’s ashes are returned, often in a decorative urn or memorial box. Some clinics offer grief counseling referrals.

    Communal Cremation: 50–150 (ashes not returned) Private Cremation: 150–400 (ashes returned in an urn)

  • Emergency Triage & Treatment

    Urinary blockages (common in male cats),Trauma (falls, car accidents) Poisoning (lilies, human medications) Respiratory distress Severe vomiting/diarrhea After-hours fees typically 1.5-2x normal rates. Includes IV fluids, pain management, and stabilization before diagnostics.

    Price:150−350 (plus treatment costs)
